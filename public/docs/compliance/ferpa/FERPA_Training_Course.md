# FERPA Training Course

## Module 1: Introduction to FERPA

### What is FERPA?

The Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA) is a federal law enacted in 1974 that:
- Protects the privacy of student education records
- Applies to all schools receiving federal funding
- Gives parents rights over their children's records (transfers to students at age 18)

### Why FERPA Matters

- Legal compliance requirement
- Protects student privacy
- Builds trust with learners
- Avoids costly penalties

---

## Module 2: Understanding Education Records

### What Qualifies as an Education Record?

Records that are:
- Directly related to a student
- Maintained by an educational institution

**Examples:**
- Grades and transcripts
- Course enrollment data
- Financial information
- Disciplinary records
- Email communications about students

### What is NOT an Education Record?

- Sole possession records (personal notes)
- Law enforcement records
- Employment records (unless tied to student status)
- Medical/treatment records

---

## Module 3: Directory Information

### Definition

Information that would not generally be considered harmful if disclosed:
- Name
- Address
- Email
- Enrollment status
- Degrees earned

### Important Notes

- Students can opt out of directory information disclosure
- Always check opt-out status before sharing
- When in doubt, treat as protected

---

## Module 4: Legitimate Educational Interest

### Who Can Access Records?

School officials with a "legitimate educational interest":
- Teachers with students in their classes
- Advisors working with students
- Administrators performing job duties
- IT staff maintaining systems

### The Need-to-Know Principle

Only access records necessary for your job function.

---

## Module 5: Best Practices

### Do's

- Verify identity before sharing information
- Use secure methods for transmitting records
- Log out of systems when not in use
- Report suspected violations immediately
- Shred physical documents with student data

### Don'ts

- Share login credentials
- Discuss student information in public areas
- Leave records visible on screens
- Send unencrypted student data via email
- Access records out of curiosity

---

## Module 6: Violations and Consequences

### Types of Violations

- Unauthorized access to records
- Improper disclosure of information
- Failure to maintain security
- Ignoring opt-out requests

### Consequences

- Disciplinary action
- Termination
- Legal liability
- Institutional penalties
