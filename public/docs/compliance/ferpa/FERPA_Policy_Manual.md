# FERPA Policy Manual

## Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA) Compliance

### Overview

The Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA) is a federal law that protects the privacy of student education records. This manual outlines Elevate LMS's policies and procedures for FERPA compliance.

### Key Principles

1. **Student Rights**
   - Right to inspect and review education records
   - Right to request amendment of records
   - Right to consent to disclosures of personally identifiable information
   - Right to file complaints with the U.S. Department of Education

2. **Protected Information**
   - Student grades and transcripts
   - Course enrollment information
   - Financial aid records
   - Disciplinary records
   - Personal identifiers (SSN, student ID)

### Data Handling Procedures

#### Collection
- Collect only necessary educational data
- Obtain consent before collecting sensitive information
- Clearly communicate data usage purposes

#### Storage
- Encrypt all student records at rest
- Implement role-based access controls
- Maintain audit logs of all access

#### Disclosure
- Verify identity before releasing records
- Document all disclosures
- Obtain written consent for third-party sharing

### Training Requirements

All staff with access to student records must complete:
- Initial FERPA training within 30 days of hire
- Annual refresher training
- Specialized training for data administrators

### Incident Response

In case of a potential FERPA violation:
1. Report immediately to the Privacy Officer
2. Document the incident
3. Assess the scope of exposure
4. Notify affected students if required
5. Implement corrective measures
